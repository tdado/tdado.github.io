<div style="max-width: 600px; margin: 0 auto; text-align: left; line-height: 1.6;">
Each neuron is picky: it responds to specific patterns in a specific part of the visual field. This is characterized by its receptive field (RF), describing <b>where</b> it looks and <b>what</b> it looks for. With sparse noise, we can find where the neuron responds to (left). Or, we optimize a random image via a neural encoder to maximally excite the neuron, and <span style="background-color: #e6f4ea; padding: 2px 4px;">get the stimulus that our neuron "likes" most</span> (right).
</div>
